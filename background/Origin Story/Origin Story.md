## The Origin of Waldo  
  
Waldo is a Toyota Tacoma TRD Off-Road that was never particularly interested in staying close to home.  
  
At first, the signs were subtle:  
* prolonged staring at maps,  
* an unhealthy attraction to dotted lines,  
* and a growing suspicion that pavement was limiting its potential.  
  
Things escalated quickly.  
  
In 2025, after burning through the factory Wildpeaks in pursuit of increasingly remote destinations, Waldo reached the Arctic Ocean for the first time. Most vehicles would have considered this a successful conclusion to their expeditionary ambitions.  
  
Waldo interpreted it as a scouting trip.  
  
Shortly afterward, the Wildpeaks were retired in honorable service and replaced with a fresh set of BFGoodrich mounted white letters out — a decision Waldo refers to as “formal expedition attire.”  
  
Combined with the Tune Outdoor habitat module, this transformation appears to have permanently altered Waldo’s psychological state.  
Since then, Waldo has developed:  
* a compulsive attraction to gravel roads,  
* an inability to ignore mountain silhouettes,  
* and a deeply concerning enthusiasm for routes described as “seasonal,” “unmaintained,” or “probably fine.”  
  
Experts believe Waldo is driven by an ancient migratory instinct shared by few modern vehicles:  
  
the irresistible need to keep heading toward colder, emptier, wilder places.  
  
Now, in 2026, Waldo is once again preparing for northern migration.  
  
Primary objectives remain:  
* reach the Arctic,  
* locate remote campsites,  
* consume vast quantities of gravel,  
* and investigate roads that inspire reasonable doubt.  
  
Secondary objectives include:  
* maintaining morale,  
* minimizing rock chips,  
* and locating fuel stations before the situation becomes philosophical.  
  
Despite repeated exposure to mud, isolation, ferry systems, and questionable decision-making, Waldo remains operational and emotionally committed to the mission.  
Further sightings expected soon.
